How Much Does an AI Audit Actually Cost?

SignalARC — April 2026 — 8 min read

Search for “AI audit” and you'll see prices that range from “free consultation” to “starts at $25,000.” Which one is right? What's actually different between them? And how do you know if you're getting your money's worth?

This guide breaks down the four real pricing tiers in the 2026 AI audit market, what you actually get at each one, and which bracket is the right fit for a small business. No fluff. Just the numbers and what they buy you.

Quick Answer

AI audits range from free to $25,000+. The productized small-business tier has converged around $999 for a 48-hour fixed-scope assessment. Free audits are usually sales calls. The $200–$500 tier is thin on specifics. The $2,000–$5,000 tier adds vertical expertise or implementation planning. Enterprise AI consulting starts around $10,000 and goes up from there. For 90% of small businesses, the $999 productized tier is the right choice.

The Short Answer: Four Pricing Tiers

The market has stabilized into four clear brackets in 2026. Each targets a different buyer with a different need.

Free
“Complimentary consultation”

Free AI Audits

Typically offered by agencies or consultants as lead generation. A 30-minute call and sometimes a short written summary.

What you usually get:

  • A call focused on qualifying you as a buyer, not diagnosing your business
  • Generic recommendations that aren't specific to your workflow
  • A pitch for the paid service at the end

Best for: Nobody, really. The exceptions are (a) a free audit from a trusted peer or (b) an early consultant building case studies in exchange for a testimonial.

$200–$500
Early-career solo consultants

Entry-Tier Paid Audits

Usually offered by consultants building their book of business. Real effort goes in, but the report is often thin — a short PDF, 3 to 4 tool recommendations, and a follow-up email.

What you usually get:

  • A genuine discovery call (30 to 45 minutes)
  • A 3 to 5 page written summary
  • Tool suggestions without implementation detail
  • Limited or no post-delivery support

Best for: Very small businesses (solopreneurs, one-person agencies) on tight budgets. You can still get value here, especially if the consultant is sharp — but you'll do more of the implementation translation yourself.

The fastest-growing segment in 2026. Fixed-scope, fixed-price, fixed-deliverable audits built around repeatable processes. The sweet spot of depth versus cost for small business owners.

What you get:

Vertical specialists

Vertical & Extended-Scope Audits

Consultants specializing in a single industry (real estate, wedding venues, accounting firms, healthcare practices) or audits that include light implementation planning. The premium is for depth, not scope.

What you get:

  • Industry-specific benchmarks and tool recommendations
  • Longer discovery process, sometimes including a team interview
  • More detailed implementation roadmap — often with SOPs or templates
  • 2 to 3 review sessions rather than one

Best for: Established businesses in a specific vertical that have already looked at generalist options and want someone who knows their industry deeply.

$10,000–$50,000+
Enterprise AI consulting

Enterprise-Tier Engagements

The AI strategy engagements built for mid-market and enterprise clients. Multi-month, multi-stakeholder, scope-flexible. Typically sold to companies with dedicated budgets and internal champions.

What you get:

  • Executive interviews across leadership and key departments
  • Multi-week strategic analysis with a full consulting team
  • A roadmap for AI adoption at the organizational level
  • Governance, security, and compliance considerations
  • Change management framework

Best for: Companies with 200+ employees, complex systems, or regulated environments. Overkill for a typical small business by a factor of 10x.

Why Is the Price Range So Wide?

The 25x spread between the entry tier and the enterprise tier isn't arbitrary. Four variables drive it.

1. Who does the work. A solo consultant pricing at $500 is billing their own time. An enterprise engagement at $25,000 has a team behind it with project managers, analysts, and senior partners — each with their own rate.

2. How much discovery gets done. A $500 audit is one call. A $25,000 audit can include 15 interviews across a leadership team plus document review. The depth of the diagnosis drives the depth of the recommendations.

3. How much of the deliverable is productized. The productized $999 tier is cost-effective because the process is systematized. Voice AI runs the discovery. Structured prompts generate the analysis. Human review polishes the output. This cuts 80% of the labor without cutting the quality.

4. Whether implementation is included. An audit that includes “we'll build the top three recommendations for you” costs more than one that just hands over the plan — because building is harder than planning.

What Should Be Included at Any Price Point

Regardless of bracket, a good AI audit has four non-negotiable components. If any of these is missing, the audit isn't worth what you're paying.

  1. A structured discovery process. Random questions aren't enough. The auditor needs a framework for pulling out specific pain points, not just generalities like “we're busy.”
  2. Specific tool recommendations. Not “use AI for email.” Actual named tools, with monthly cost and setup time attached, mapped to specific workflows you discussed.
  3. Quantified impact. How many hours per week does each recommendation save? What's the monthly cost? What's the net ROI? If these numbers aren't in the report, the recommendations are guesses.
  4. A path to implementation. A report that says “use these seven tools” without saying which one to start with or how to set it up is 20% of the value. The prioritization and sequencing are most of the value.

Red Flags to Watch For at Any Price

  • A generic list of tools. If the recommendations could be copy-pasted from a blog post, the audit wasn't custom.
  • No numbers. An audit without hours-saved and dollars-saved estimates is a list of opinions, not an analysis.
  • “We'll need more calls to quote you.” Scope creep is baked in. A productized audit gives you the full price upfront.
  • No money-back guarantee. Any auditor confident in their deliverable should stand behind it.
  • Recommendations that require hiring the auditor. If every recommendation conveniently leads to an expensive implementation contract, the audit is a sales funnel — not an unbiased analysis.

Is an AI Audit Worth It?

Run the math on your own business. The breakeven is easier than most people think.

If an audit identifies 2 hours per week of reclaimable time and your effective hourly rate (or opportunity cost) is $100, that's $800 per month in recaptured time. A $999 audit pays back in about 5 weeks.

Most businesses recover far more than 2 hours. The average SignalARC client recovers 5 to 10 hours per week after implementing the recommendations. At $100 per hour, that's $2,000 to $4,000 per month in value — against $40 to $100 per month in tool subscriptions. The math strongly favors the audit for any business where the owner's time is worth more than $50 per hour.

The math doesn't work at enterprise prices for small businesses. A $25,000 audit that identifies 10 hours per week takes 25 weeks to pay back at $100 per hour. That's viable for a company with a dedicated budget, but usually not for an owner-operator.

  • 5–50 employees, owner-operator, wants a clear plan: $999 productized tier (SignalARC's AI Assessment)
  • Established business in a specific vertical, already looked at generalists: $2,500–$5,000 vertical specialist
  • 200+ employees, regulated environment, needs a strategic framework: Enterprise consulting tier

The price range exists because the market serves very different buyers.
